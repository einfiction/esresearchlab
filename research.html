Research

Our research at Eindorff & Synthoris Research Lab focuses on the Combined Theoretical Signal (CTS) model, a pioneering approach to integrate gravity and quantum physics. We utilize pulsar-sourced gravitational wave data and strain data from LIGO detectors to validate our models.

Current Projects

  • Combined Theoretical Signal (CTS): A validated universal model integrating gravity and quantum physics, addressing the fundamental mechanisms of the universe.
    • The initial CTS manuscript (v1) - DOI: 10.20944/preprints202405.1233.v1
    • Enhanced and validated v1 model with expanded Cosmic Mechanism explanations - DOI: 10.20944/preprints202405.1233.v3
    • Final CTS v2 with high precision validation and enhanced mathematical model - DOI: 10.20944/preprints202405.1233.v4
  • Pulsar Data Analysis: Utilizing pulsar timing arrays to detect gravitational waves and enhance the CTS model's precision.
  • Quantum Fluctuations: Investigating the role of quantum fluctuations and higher-dimensional effects in cosmic structures and their implications for theoretical physics.
  • Dark Matter Influence: Exploring how dark matter contributes to the formation of massive black holes and the overall structure of the universe.

Future Validation Work: Critical Cosmic Phenomena

  • Neutrinos: Top candidate due to their fundamental role in particle physics and potential to explain dark matter.
  • Dark Matter: Validation to understand its distribution and interaction.
  • Gravitational Waves: Further validation using different datasets to confirm the robustness of CTS.
  • Black Holes: Studying their role in the cosmic mechanism, including quantum properties.
